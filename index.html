(1) What is the independent variable? What is the dependent variable?¶

The independent variable is the words condition, either congruent (the word is a color, and the ink/font color of the word matches) or incongruent (the ink/font color of the word does not match).

Examples:

  • Congruent: RED
  • Incongruent: ORANGE

The dependent variable is the amount of time it takes someone to state the color of the ink/font for each word condition.

(2) What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? Specify your null and alternative hypotheses, and clearly define any notation used. Justify your choices.¶

Data:

The data contains the results from 24 people who were timed at how long it took them to state the ink/font color from both congruent and incongruent words.

When looking at the data, we can make several assumptions:

  1. The times recorded are continuous data
  2. Each participant in the test is independent of each other
  3. Each participant recorded a time for both word conditions, therefore the data is dependent and considered paired
  4. The times recorded (dependent variable) are normallly distributed


Hypotheses:

I'm basing my hypothesis on the idea that we do not know if a person can identify the color of an incongruent word faster or slower than a congruent word, or if the word condition has no effect on the time. Therefore, to cover all possibilities:

$$\Large H_0: \mu_{Incongruent} - \mu_{Congruent} = 0$$

$$\Large H_1: \mu_{Incongruent} - \mu_{Congruent} \neq 0$$

The Null hypothesis is that the amount of time it takes someone to state the ink/font color of an incongruent word is the same as stating a congruent word. This is denoted by subtracting the hypothetical population mean of the Congruent column from the hypothetical population mean of the Incongruent column and the difference being equal.

The Alternative hypothesis is that the amount of time it takes someone to state the ink/font color of an incongruent word is different (longer or shorter) than stating a congruent word. This is denoted by subtracting the hypothetical population mean of the Congruent column from the hypothetical population mean of the Incongruent column and the difference being greater or less than zero.


Test:

Based on this information, I will perform a dependent t-test for paired samples using the mean difference. A t-test is appropriate because the sample size is under 30, and it will help reduce some of the bias with the small sample size. Since the alternative hypothesis contain a $\neq$, this will be a two-tailed test.

Test Setup

Confidence Interval

The confidence interval I am using is a standard 95%. Because this is a two-tailed test, the 5% will be split in half to cover both sides of the distribution. The upper bound will be 0.975, which will be used when calculating the t-critical value.

$\normalsize \alpha = 0.05$

Degrees of Freedom

This is used to calculate the t-critical value.

The sample size: $\normalsize n = 24$

Degrees of Freedom: $\normalsize n - 1$

Degrees of Freedom: $\normalsize 24 - 1 = 23$

T-Critical Value

Calculating the t-critical value below. This could also be looked up in a t-table.

Statistical Test

The appropriate test for this scenario is the Dependent t-test for paired samples.


$\Huge t = \frac{\bar x_{D}\ - \ \mu_{0}}{\frac{s_{D}}{\sqrt{n}}}$


Where:

  • x bar ($\bar x_{D}$) is the mean difference of 7.964791666666667
  • mu naught ($\mu_{0}$) is null hypothesis of zero
  • Standard deviation of the difference in means ($s_{D}$) is 4.864826910359056
  • Square root of n ($\sqrt{n}$) is 24, the number of values in sample

Test Results

The t-statistic of 8.0207 far exceeds the critical value of 2.0687. Additionally, the p-value of 4.1030e-08 is extremely small and is statistically significant. Therefore, we reject the Null hypothesis.

The low p-value tells us that the results did not occur by chance, meaning that the times for incongruent words will almost always be higher than those for congruent words.

Conclusion: The people in the study can read words faster than they can extrapolate the color of the letters in the word. This result was expected.
